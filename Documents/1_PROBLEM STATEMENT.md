# PROBLEM STATEMENT

# AI-Powered Insurance for Gig Delivery Workers

# 

# Delivery partners working for platforms like Zomato, Swiggy, Zepto, Amazon, and Dunzo are an important part of India’s digital economy. They deliver food, groceries, and packages to millions of customers every day. Most of these workers earn money based on the number of deliveries they complete. This means their income depends on how many hours they are able to work.

# 

# However, delivery workers often face situations where they cannot work because of external problems that are not under their control. For example, heavy rain, floods, extreme heat, or very high pollution can make it difficult or unsafe to deliver orders. Sometimes curfews, strikes, or sudden area closures also stop delivery services in certain locations. When these disruptions happen, delivery partners lose working hours and cannot earn their usual daily income.

# 

# In many cases, these disruptions can reduce a worker’s weekly earnings by 20–30%. Even though this is a common problem, there is currently no insurance product that protects delivery workers from losing income during these events. Most existing insurance plans only cover health, accidents, or vehicle damage. They do not help when a worker simply cannot work because of bad weather or other external disruptions.

# 

# Because of this, gig workers have no financial safety net when such events occur. They must handle the loss of income on their own, which can make their financial situation unstable.

# 

# To solve this problem, we propose building an AI-powered parametric insurance platform designed specifically for delivery workers. This platform will monitor external factors such as weather conditions, pollution levels, and other disruptions. When these conditions reach certain levels that prevent delivery work, the system will automatically trigger an insurance claim.

# 

# The platform will also use AI to calculate fair weekly insurance premiums based on risk factors like delivery location, past disruption patterns, and worker activity. Once a disruption is detected and verified, the system will automatically process a payout to compensate the worker for the income they lost.

# 

# The insurance will follow a simple weekly pricing model so that it matches the weekly earning cycle of gig workers. The goal of this solution is to provide a simple, automated, and reliable way to protect delivery workers from income loss caused by events they cannot control.
